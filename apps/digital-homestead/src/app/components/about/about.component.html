Digital Homesteading is the practice of building an online home you can actually keep. That means choosing tools you control, publishing under your own name, and growing relationships that do not vanish when a platform changes the rules.

It does not require a server rack in your basement on day one. It starts with practical ownership: your own site, your own mailing list, your own tools, and a clearer understanding of how your digital life fits together.

The goal is resilience, not purity. You learn what to own now, what to host later, and how to grow alongside neighbors building a more durable web.

Publish from a place you control so your audience can keep finding you even when major platforms shift.

Move away from default platforms and toward a stack that matches your values, budget, and technical comfort.

Start small, document what matters, and create systems you can maintain without burning out.

Learn from people taking similar steps into self-hosting, publishing, and community-first digital life.

You do not need to do everything at once. Pick the next level of ownership that fits your current season, then build outward from there.
