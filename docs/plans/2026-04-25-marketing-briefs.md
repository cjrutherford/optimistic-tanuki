# Marketing Briefs

## Summary
This document captures internal marketing guidance for `forgeofwill`,
`digital-homestead`, `christopherrutherford-net`, `leads-app`, and Towne
Square (`local-hub`). It is not final web copy. It defines audience, promise,
proof, and the content gaps that future public-facing pages should address.

## Forge of Will
- Audience:
  individuals and small teams managing projects, delivery, planning, and
  execution.
- Primary promise:
  turn plans into visible progress with a focused workspace that feels active
  and directed.
- Supporting proof:
  project/task workflows, risk and journal surfaces, timer features, and the
  already cinematic product framing.
- Hero direction:
  ambition, momentum, and progress under control.
- Current content gap:
  needs clearer plain-language benefit statements for non-technical visitors,
  not just evocative visual branding.

## Digital Homestead
- Audience:
  people exploring self-hosting, digital ownership, creative independence, and
  community-first online life.
- Primary promise:
  build a durable online home that you control.
- Supporting proof:
  resource sections, benefits, community material, and educational framing
  already present in the landing content.
- Hero direction:
  practical autonomy with welcoming community support.
- Current content gap:
  needs tighter proof of “how this helps” beyond philosophy so visitors quickly
  understand the concrete path from interest to action.

## ChristopherRutherford.net
- Audience:
  potential consulting clients, collaborators, and organizations looking for
  senior engineering help.
- Primary promise:
  independent technical leadership for self-hosted, maintainable software
  systems.
- Supporting proof:
  services grid, project portfolio, broad technology coverage, and delivery
  background in the about section.
- Hero direction:
  expert credibility, ownership, and durable delivery.
- Current content gap:
  needs stronger explicit positioning for who should engage, what outcomes are
  delivered, and why the approach differs from generic development agencies.

## Leads App
- Audience:
  solo operators, agencies, and small sales teams doing discovery and
  qualification work.
- Primary promise:
  move from onboarding to discovery topics to active lead management without
  losing context.
- Supporting proof:
  onboarding interview, topic generation/confirmation, lead dashboard, active
  topic counts, and analytics hooks.
- Hero direction for a future landing page:
  “configure discovery once, then work the pipeline with clear signals.”
- Current content gap:
  there is no clear public-facing marketing surface that explains the workflow
  from topic discovery to lead action in one narrative.

## Towne Square / Local Hub
- Audience:
  residents, local organizers, moderators, and community builders who want a
  local-first digital commons.
- Primary promise:
  bring neighborhood commerce, communication, and community participation into
  one local platform.
- Supporting proof:
  city/community browsing, classifieds, direct messaging, and donation/funding
  goals.
- Hero direction:
  local connection, civic usefulness, and trusted neighborhood-scale exchange.
- Current content gap:
  the landing page explains features well, but future marketing material should
  sharpen the emotional reason to join: belonging, local trust, and useful
  everyday coordination.

## Recommended Next Copy Work
- Build one concise homepage message architecture for each app:
  audience, promise, proof, CTA.
- Add one “how it works” band for `leads-app` and `local-hub`.
- Add one “who this is for” band for `christopherrutherford-net`.
- Add one “start here” content path for `digital-homestead`.
