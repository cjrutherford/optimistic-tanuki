# HAI Marketing Brief

## Summary

This brief defines the public-facing message architecture for `hai`. HAI is
the brand shorthand for **Hopeful Aspirations Industries**. The site should
behave like the public company front door for a repo-backed software business:
software delivery, cloud architecture, personal-cloud systems, and a visible
product ecosystem where the repository is a primary company asset and
production vehicle.

## Audience

- Founders and owner-operators who need senior engineering help without a large
  agency layer.
- Small teams and organizations building or stabilizing software and cloud
  systems.
- Individuals and families exploring owned compute, self-hosting, and durable
  personal-cloud infrastructure.

## Primary Promise

- Build software, cloud, and personal-cloud systems that stay legible as they
  grow.

## Supporting Proof

- Delivery experience across applications, platform workflows, APIs, and
  integration-heavy product work.
- Cloud architecture focused on service boundaries, maintainability, and
  operational clarity.
- Personal-cloud and HAI Computer system work grounded in ownership,
  self-hosting, backups, local AI, and practical home infrastructure.
- A visible ecosystem of products and repository-backed work that demonstrates
  execution, not just consulting language.

## Hero Direction

- Serious, credible, and ownership-oriented.
- Lead with who HAI helps, what HAI delivers, and why the approach is
  different.
- Introduce the full name `Hopeful Aspirations Industries` near the top of the
  page.
- Make it clear that the repository and shipped apps are part of the operating
  business, not a side portfolio.

## CTA Strategy

- Primary CTA: `Start a Project`
- Secondary CTA: `Explore HAI Computer Systems`
- Supporting path: browse the HAI ecosystem later in the page.

## Content Gaps To Address

- The hero should not rely on playful acronym expansion to explain the brand.
- Services need clearer audience and outcome framing before philosophy or
  ecosystem exploration.
- Personal-cloud and HAI Computer content should read as concrete proof, not
  abstract aspiration.
- The playful acronym generator can remain, but it must be clearly separated
  from the real company name.
